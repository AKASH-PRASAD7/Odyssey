## Vectors

**Definition:** A vector is a quantity that has both magnitude (length) and direction. It is represented as an arrow in space.

**Notation:**
$$ \vec{v} = \begin{bmatrix} v_1 \\ v_2 \\ v_3 \end{bmatrix} $$

**Magnitude:**
$$ ||\vec{v}|| = \sqrt{v_1^2 + v_2^2 + v_3^2} $$

**Direction:**
$$ \theta = \arctan\left(\frac{v_2}{v_1}\right) $$

**Example:**
$$ \vec{v} = \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 4 \end{bmatrix} $$

**Magnitude:**
$$ ||\vec{v}|| = \sqrt{3^2 + 4^2} = \sqrt{9 + 16} = \sqrt{25} = 5 $$

**Direction:**
$$ \theta = \arctan\left(\frac{4}{3}\right) \approx 53.13^\circ $$

**Note:** This method is particularly useful for finding the magnitude and direction of a vector.

## Vector Operations

**Definition:** Vector operations are mathematical operations that are performed on vectors. They are used to find the magnitude and direction of a vector.

**Notation:**
$$ \vec{v} = \begin{bmatrix} v_1 \\ v_2 \\ v_3 \end{bmatrix} $$

**Magnitude:**
$$ ||\vec{v}|| = \sqrt{v_1^2 + v_2^2 + v_3^2} $$

**Direction:**
$$ \theta = \arctan\left(\frac{v_2}{v_1}\right) $$

**Example:**
$$ \vec{v} = \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 4 \end{bmatrix} $$

**Magnitude:**
$$ ||\vec{v}|| = \sqrt{3^2 + 4^2} = \sqrt{9 + 16} = \sqrt{25} = 5 $$

**Direction:**
$$ \theta = \arctan\left(\frac{4}{3}\right) \approx 53.13^\circ $$

**Addition:**
$$ \vec{v} + \vec{w} = \begin{bmatrix} v_1 + w_1 \\ v_2 + w_2 \\ v_3 + w_3 \end{bmatrix} $$

**Subtraction:**
$$ \vec{v} - \vec{w} = \begin{bmatrix} v_1 - w_1 \\ v_2 - w_2 \\ v_3 - w_3 \end{bmatrix} $$

**Multiplication by a scalar:**
$$ c\vec{v} = \begin{bmatrix} cv_1 \\ cv_2 \\ cv_3 \end{bmatrix} $$

**Note:** This method is particularly useful for finding the magnitude and direction of a vector.
